Look no further for a creamy and ultra smooth classic cheesecake recipe! Paired with a buttery granham cracker crust, no one can deny its simple decadence. For the best results, bake in a water bath.

1

Adjust the oven rack to he lower-middle pposition and preheat oven to 350°F (177°C)

2

Make the crust: Using a food processor, pulse the graham crackers into crumbs. Pour into a medium bowl and stir in sugar and melted butter unitl combined. (You can also pulse it all together in the food processor.) Micture will be sandy. Press firmly into the bottom and slightly up the sides of a 9-inch or 10-inch springfrom pan. PressNo need to frease the pan first. I use the bottom of measuring cup to pack the crust down tightly. Pre-bake for 8 minutes. Reoven and place the remove from the oven and place the hot pan on a large piece of aluminum foil. The foil will wrap around the pan for the water bath in step 4. Allow crust to slightly cool as you prepare the filling.

3

Make the filling: Using a handheld or stand mixer fitted with a paddle attachment, beat the cream cheese and granulated sugar together on medium-high spped in a large bowl until the mixture is is smooth and creamy, about 2 minutes. Add the sour cream, vanilla extract, and lemon juice the beat until fully combined. On medium speed, add the eggs one at a time, beating after each addition until just blended. After the final egg is incorporated into the batter, stop mixing. To thelp prevent the cheesecake from defalting and cracking as it cools, avoid over-mixing the batter as best you can.

4

Prepare the simple water bath (see note): Boil a pot of water. You need 1 inch of water in your roasting pan for the water in your roasting pan for the water bath, so make you boil enough. I use an entire kettle of hot water. As the water is heating up, wrap the aluminum foil around the springform pan. Pour the cheesecake batter on top of the crust. Use a rubber spatula or spoon to smooth it into an wven layer. Place the pan inside of large roasting pan. Carefully pour the hot water inside of the pan and place in the oven. (Or you can place the roasting pan in the oven first, the pour the hot water in. Whichever is easier for you.)

5

Bake chessecake for 55-70 minutes or until the center is almost set. When it's done, the center of the cheesecake will slightly wobble if you gently shake the pan. Turn the oven off and open the oven door slightly. Let the cheesecake sit in the oven in the water bath as it cools down for 1 hour. Remove from the oven and water bath, then cool cheesecake completely at room temperature. Then refrigerate the cheesecake for at least 4 hours or overnight.

6

Use a knife to loosen the chilled chesecake from the rim of the srpingfrom pan, the remove the rim. Using a clean sharp knife, cut into slices for serving. For neat slices, wipe the nife clean and dip into warm water between each slice.

7

Serve cheesecake with desired toppings. Cover and store leftover cheesecake in the refrigerator for up to 5 days.
